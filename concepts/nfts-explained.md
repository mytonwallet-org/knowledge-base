# NFTs Explained

## What Is an NFT?

An NFT (Non-Fungible Token) is a unique digital item recorded on the blockchain. Unlike regular tokens (where each token is identical), each NFT is one-of-a-kind. Think of it like a digital certificate of ownership for a specific item.

## What Can Be an NFT?

- **Digital art** — images, animations, videos
- **Collectibles** — trading cards, game items
- **Telegram Gifts** — gifts sent in Telegram
- **Domain names** — TON DNS domains (like `yourname.ton`)
- **Membership passes** — access to communities or services
- **MTW Cards** — branded wallet collectibles

## Collections

NFTs are usually organized into **collections** — groups of related items created by the same author or project. For example, all Telegram Gifts form a collection, and all TON DNS domains form another.

## Ownership

When you own an NFT, the blockchain records your address as the owner. You can:
- **View** it in your wallet gallery
- **Send** it to another address (transfer ownership)
- **Sell** it on a marketplace (like Getgems for TON or Magic Eden for Solana)
- **Burn** it (permanently destroy it)

## Are NFTs Valuable?

Value depends entirely on demand. Some NFTs are highly sought after and trade for large amounts. Others (like spam NFTs airdropped to your wallet) are worthless. The app lets you hide unwanted NFTs.

## NFTs in MyTonWallet

Your NFTs appear in the Assets section of the Home tab, organized by collection. You can view attributes, send them, or manage visibility. TON DNS domains and Telegram Gifts are handled as special NFT types with additional functionality (domain linking, renewal, etc.).
